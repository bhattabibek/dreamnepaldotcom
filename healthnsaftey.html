Health and saftey


Common Health Problems in Nepal

Nepal is a Himalayan Kingdom with varied altitude and climate. Nepal is a developing country so it is quite natural to suffer from some illness after coming to Nepal. The most common Health Problems are: - Diarrhea - Common Cold - Fever - Cough Diarrhea

It is also known as Traveller’s Diarrhea. Pathogens, which may be responsible are - Salmonella - Vibrio Cholera - Shigella - E.Coli - Most common – Viral - Commonly no organism is identified - Mostly it is of viral origin and it is self – limited. No medication is required.

An attack of diarrhea lasting 2 – 5 days commonly affects travelers, particularly affect, when visiting developing countries. The onset is usually abrupt and the stool is watery, Abdominal cramps, Anorexia and vomiting are common and there may be fever.

The disorder resolves spontaneously. Anti-diarraheal agents are best avoided. If prolonged or very serious then medication may be needed. Anti - biotics are not necessary for the mild attacks, especially as they induce antibiotic resistance.

Severe attacks can be aborted in most cases with the help of anti – biotic. Dehydration should be prevented by drinking of non – alcoholic fluide. Use of oral rehydration supplements.

Prevention

Good Hygienic practice. Drinking clean water: In Nepal, water is the most important media for the spread of diseases and to get rid of the diseases, water should be boiled for about 15 minutes and it is the most easiest way for the prevention.


- It is best advice able to drink boiled and filtered water until your stay in Nepal. - Avoid uncooked vegetables, meat. - Common cold, fever and cough can also be prevented by drinking boiled water. If very severe then only medication is needed. If you visit other parts of Nepal especially the Tarai regions, you may have to be aware of the disease like Malaria, which is spread fast by means of Mosquitoes. It is better to take prevention of Mosquitoes even in Katmandu.

If you are an Asthmathic patient, pollution in Katmandu may directly affect your health status and better to use mask or if you avoid it or continue your medication in which you are under. If you get injury do not forget to take anti - tetanus within 4 hours of an injury. Do not take injection anywhere, where sterile precautions are not taken. Use disposable syringe for such situation. Have cleanly washed fresh fruits. Flies are also important media for disease spreading so keep your surroundings especially rooms clean. Drink only boiled water/milk.
