CulturalTours

ANAKPURDHAM


Teral’s most fascinating city,historically called Mithila,more commonly Janakpur presently the headquarter of both janakpur Zone and Dhanusha district,was the capital of King janak’s ancient mithila kingdom during the Trela Yog or Period,nearly 12,000 years ago.An important pilgrimage site for Hindus, Janakpurdham is dominated by the impressive Janaki Temple.an obligatory stop on the Hindu Pilgrimage circuit Janakpurdham,has its own language and script.The most important historical reference to mithila /Janakpurdham is the Hindu epic of Ramayana.The Maithali languages is widely spoken in the area.Janakpurdham is very famous for fish as the city is well known as city of pounds” NOTABLETEMPLES OF JANAKPURDHAM JANAKI TEMPLE (MANDIR) This magnificient temple of marble was built by Brishbhanu kunwari,wife of king pratap singh of Orchha Kingdom of Madhya Pradesh of India.This temple takes inspiration from Mugal architecture and is the biggest temple of Nepal.On the four corner of this temple ,cupolas enhance its architecture supremacy.The middle portion of the east side is known as “Sheesh Mahal”And at the centre of the inner part where the idols of Sita and Ram are housed in called the Janaki Mahal Behind the Janaki Mahal is the Janak Sunaiyana Mandir dedicated to the father and mother of Goddess Sita.The whole temple complex is also locally knows as the Naulakha Temple as Nine Lakh rupees,or Rs.199,000-a colossal sum of money then where said to have been spent in the construction of the Janaki north east corner lies the Lakshman temple,while the manginificient vivah Mandap lies to the southeast in the midst of the beautiful garden.In the southeast corner is a park while a dharmashal (Rest house)stands to the south of Janak Mandir.

SHREE RAM MANDIR:


This pagoda temple was built in 1782 by Amar Singh Thapa,while its glide roof was bestowed by Rana Prime Minister Chandra Shumsher in 1907.This temple,which lies on the western banks of the holy Dhanush sagar and to the southeast of shree Janak Mandir, abounds in ancient idols.

SHREE JANAK MANDIR:


This temple at Janak Chowk is dedicated to king Janak renewed for his scholarship in that age.He was also regarded as “videh”or someone without a body.Though this temple is comparatively small,it has held great importance in popularizing Janakpurdham as the ancient capital city of vedic Mithila Kingdom.

DULHA-DULHIN(bridegroom &bride)MANDIR:


This temple lies to the west of the Ramananda Chowk,where a magnificent gateway stands with an idol of shankaracharya on the top.The temple enshrines the idols of Goddess Sita and Lord Ram dressed as bride and bridegroom.

SHREE SHANKAT MOCHAN MANDIR & RANGABHOOMI:

This temple is dedicated to Hanuman,the most loyal attendant of Lord Ram &Goddess Sita.Hanuman,The son the wind God,is also knoen by the name of sankat mochan,or redeemer of all woes.This tempole lies in the northwest corner of the mythological and called “Rangabhoomi” is also known as “Barah Bigha” as its is 12bighas (1 hectare=1.6 bighas ) in area.it was here that king Janak arraged the Dhanuskhyagya.or the Bow-Breaking ceremony,in the Treta Yug.This Hanuman temple sees thousands of devotee twice a week Thuesday and Saturday.

ALESHWARNATH MANDIR



This temple lies in jaleshwar, the headquarters of Mahottain district,18km to the southwest of janakpurdham.The temple inshirines a shiva lingam 20 feet below the surface.The phallic symbol of Lord Shiva lies immersed in water hence its namesake.The temple draws large numbers of devotees from both Nepal and India on Shivaratri,the Night of Lord shiva ,in February,and also during the mouth of shrawan (july –August) Buses leave regularly for Jaleshwar from Janakpurdham and Bardibas.

DHANUSHADHAM


This place of mythological importance lies 20km to the north of janakpurdham.It was here that a fragment of shiva’s divine bow fell after Lord Ram broke it into three pieces during the Bow-Breaking Ceremony held at Rangabhoomi in janakpurdham a condition for obtaining the hand of sita in marriage- During the Maker Mela festival held every Sunday in the mouth of Magh (January –February), hundreds of thousands of devotees throng Dhanushaadham to pay homage to the fossilized bow piece .Buses leave regularly for Dhanushadham from janakpurdham.One can also reach there from Bhiman to East West-Highway –a distance of just 6 km.

SHREE LAXMI NARAYAN MANDIR (MATIHANI)


This temle lies on the north bank of the huge Laxmi Sagar Pond.It is 6 km from jaleshwar to the south.It was in this pond that the matakor (soil digging ceremony)was performed before the marriage of Sita to Ram .There is also a Laxmi Narayan Temple in the area Another temple here is know for an iron strap used by a hermit called Tasmaiya Baba,with engravings on it dating back to about 450 years.

SOME IMPORTANT PONDS OF JANAKPURDHAM:


GANGASAGAR



Any pilgrimage is in complete without a ritual bath in this pond which has never dried up.According to popular belief ,water from seven seas and all sacred place were collected and this sacred pond called Ganga Sagar took shape .

DHANUSH SAGAR:


This poud lies infront of Ram Mandir.It is said that the bow which Lord Shiva had given to King Janak during theTreta Yog was kept at this place for safe keeping and the pond was built in the shape of the weapon.

RATNA SAGAR:


This pond is to the northwest of Rangabhoomi and Ramanand Chowk.It is said that King Janak used to store jewels in this pond .People say that the bow turns a shining red when observed from one corner of the pond while it disappears after prayer and lighting of the ritual flame by the priest of the local temple .

AGNIKUNDA:


This pond lies about half a kilometer to the south west of the Ratna sagar, where yagyas (offering made to a sacred fire ) used to take place during Vedic times.Although comparatively small in size to other ponds,it is very deep.

Unique cultural dance of janakpurdham:


JATA-JATIN


This dance is performed at night by a group rural women in the month of Bhadra (August/September )when there is a drought.The women catch frogs and put them in small pitches filled with a mixture of water and cow dung .The women divide themselves into two groups –one masquerading as the male which is called “jatin”The women then place the pitchers on their haeads and go about the village ,singing a song in question –answer form.At the end ,they throw the pitchers in to the courtyard of a person assumed to be evil –minded and then disperse .The cultural dance is performed with the hope that there will be rain.

JHJHYA


This festival (on the 6th day of the bright lunar fortnight in kartik ) is the most auspicious festival ofMithilanchat (Another dance worth watching as performed by a group of women every year in the mount of Ashwin (September/October) during the Dasha in festival from Ghatasthapana to vijaya dashami)

SAMACHRKEBA:


This is a play performed by the rural women ofMithilanchal for a fortnight ,starting on chhath in the mouth of Kartik (October/November) and lasting till the full moon.Through this play women express their affection to their brothers.Every evening women leave their homes with dolls In a bamboo lamp.They gather at a certain crossing and sits in a circle singing till midnight.On the final night ,they buth the moustache of “chugla” the evil doll.The musical instruments as drums and flute in the presence of their brother.

JHARRI:


This play is performed at the time of “Hamuman –jhanda”(celebration commemorating Lord Hanuman ‘s flag) “Data or Tijya”(Muslim rituals ) About 10-15 men perform this dance with a stick that is about 18 inches long.This singing and dancing go on for hours.

MITHILA PAINTINGES & FOLKARTS:


Mithila paitings are renowned the world over.There is a painting centre (janakpur women Development centre )at kuwa, janakpurdham where nearly Five dozen rural women from different villages are involved in preparing paintings of Mithila folk art for export to Western countries and other markets .

FESTIVALS:


JHOOLA:


Ritual of placing Ram and Sita on a swing starts from the third day of the brightening lunar fortnight in the main temple of janakpurdham admist cultural programmes. It is performed daily till midnight and continues till the 14th day the lunar fornight.

CHHATH:

It is celebrated six days after Deepawali with great devotion and in solemnity During the celebration there is harmony in the air,and no distinction is made between rich and poor.caste,creed and color.

MITHILAMADHYAPARIKRAMA:

(Fortnighthy circumambulation of 14 pilgrimange spots stretching 127km)takes place every year during this month through demarcated areas of Mithilanchal.The pilgrimage includes 14 place of historical and mythological importance.including four in bordering Indian territory.

HOLI:


Holi is the festival of colors.it is celebrated next day after the completion of Antargriha Parikrama .it is also called “Phagua”In local language.

ACCESS TO JANAKPURDHAM:BY Air:


There are regular fights to janakpurdham from Kathmandu.
By land:
There are regular buses to janakpurdham from different parts of Nepal or Visitors from India may arrive here by Train through jayanagar and by bus through Bhittamod,sitamadi.
